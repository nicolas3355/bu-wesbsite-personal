About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Boston University working with Prof. Eran Tromer. My research focuses on cryptography, with an emphasis on secure multiparty computation, zero-knowledge proofs, and distributed systems. I have published in top-tier venues including USENIX, PODC, and Financial Cryptography.

Prior to my current position, I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science with a specialization in applied cryptography and distributed systems. I have experience collaborating with both academic and industry partners, and have taught graduate-level courses on cryptography, blockchain, and network security.

Research Interests

  • Zero-Knowledge Proof Systems
  • Distributed Systems and Consensus
  • Verifiable Secret Sharing and Information Dispersal
  • Cryptographic Protocol Design
